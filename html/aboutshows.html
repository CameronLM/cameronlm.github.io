    About Shows

    • Astroblast

      Astroblast follows the zany adventures of a crew of space animals who run the Astroblast Space Station, the coolest hangout in the galaxy. The team of animals show through their friendship how to embrace differences, model positive relationships, and foster healthy habits to be the best good-will ambassadors in the galaxy.

    • Boj

      Boj is a bilby who lives in Giggly Park with his fun loving family. The series' educational philosophy enables children to observe the processes of scientific inquiry (known as Boj-a-Boom ideas) and technological problem-solving in action in an engaging and age-appropriate way. Additionally, kids learn about physical science concepts and seeing positive attitudes towards learning, particularly curiosity and perseverance.

    • Caillou

      The series explores growing experiences and events that resonate with all children similar to Caillou in age. Young viewers feel encouraged to imitate our little hero as they watch him go through various stages of development. Role-playing teaches children to cope with strong emotions like anger, sadness and jealousy and thereby accomplish feats they would never have thought possible. At this age, role playing is as appealing as it is essential and by using their imagination, children are empowered beyond their years and dare to try and discover new things.

    • Chloe's Closet

      The series explores the magical world of an adorable, imaginative little girl named Chloe, who embarks upon fantastical adventures during her dress-up play. Chloe's adventures involve the audience and encourage viewers to celebrate how delightful it is to be a little kid and how exciting it is to explore preschool math skills. So, get ready to turn on your imagination as you help Chloe open the closet door in her bedroom, put on a costume and embark upon a fabulously fun and fearless, anything-can-happen journey that makes perfect sense if you just make believe.

    • Clangers

      The series interweves recurring themes within the narrative ­ kindness, collaboration, family, community, and recycling ­which are all very relevant to the Clangers' world and that of young children. Great storytelling remains at the core of the show, as each episode centers on members of the Clanger family making discoveries, inventing something new, solving a problem, or greeting a new visitor to their small, blue planet. The characters are excellent role models when it comes to: investigating, improvising, interpreting, inventing and imagining.

    • Earth to Luna

      The series educational mission is to spark children's curiousity about science, and encourage them to wonder about how the world works. Luna inspires children to value science as something meaningful, accessible and fun, as she explores the impact that science has on our world, and pursues answers to scientific questions that viewers will relate to and enjoy.

    • LazyTown

      The series aims to reach out to children and adults all over the world and inspire them to become physically active. Each episode presents children with a lifestyle choice that is good for them, but without preaching or telling them what to do or what not to do. Instead, Lazytown seeks to inspire children to make positive choices for themselves, through entertainment and leading by example. The goals of the series are: to encourage children to enjoy physical activity, to motivate children to engage in sports and play, to promote other healthful habits such as proper sleep, and to empower caregivers to make positive decisions for their children that help them have active and healthy lifestyles.

    • Lily's Driftwood Bay

      The series promotes making your own fun and uncovering unexpected adventures from the things that you find. The show has five learning goals: developing observational skills while exploring other points of view, model how imagination and creativity can inspire adventures or stories, show viewers community is a resource, learn collaboration and teamwork skills and communicate that not one, but many different ideas are essential to solving problems and generating creative ideas. Lily teaches kids about living in a community, the importance of inter-generational relationships and how to use their imaginations and creativity to inspire personal adventures and tell their own stories.

    • Maya The Bee

      Maya the Bee is an adventure comedy centered on Maya, a young bee filled with curiosity, who loves to explore and discover the world. Maya and her best friend, Willy, love asking questions about everything to find out about the mysteries that surround them. Maya’s exploration encourages kids to learn facts about nature through her adventures. The series features entertaining content with lessons in bio diversity and nature protection.

    • Nina's World

      The series celebrates family, community, creativity, and global awareness. Nina embraces the big wonder of everyday moments in the world around her. While living in such a diverse community, Nina gets to experience the world without ever leaving town. Through a close relationship with her Abuelita (grandmother) and parents, family values and traditions are emphasized. Spanish words and phrases will be introduced in an organic way throughout each episode. Nina also knows that communication is the key to expressing thoughts and emotions. That¹s why she¹s eager to learn American Sign Language to communicate better with deaf friend, Nico. Wherever Nina turns, she¹s surrounded by interesting new discoveries, great friends, and the the possibilities and wonder of the world around her. Just like Nina, viewers will be inspired to creativity capture the unique and happy moments in each and every day.

    • Pajanimals

      For pre-school aged children the world is just starting to feel like a very big place with each passing day and experience. Bedtime, for example, can become more challenging as they learn to handle separation anxiety, nightmares, and irrational fears. This series tackles these developmental challenges head on providing comfort and community while modeling courage and growth. Through song and story, the series will teach the skills that preschoolers need to move through their days and nights successfully. The main teaching goals of the series are: managing big feelings, navigating transitions, solving problems, providing structure and predictability, modeling and offering tools for self-regulation, and teaching empathy.

    • Poppy Cat

      The series aims to celebrate and reinforce the incredible impact imaginative play and problem-solving have on a child's emotional and intellectual growth. The shows three primary educational goals are: to celebrate the fun and power of inventing and sharing stories, to encourage children's fantasy play, make believe and imagination as ways to solve problems and pursue their goals, and to demonstrate the rewards and challenges of making and keeping friends.

    • Ruff-Ruff, Tweet and Dave

      The series explores a broad range of early learning topics within themed adventures, introducing viewers to new words, concepts and simple numeracy through cognitive and symbolic learning. Each episode nurtures viewers' knowledge and understanding of the world as well as their physical, creative and mathematical development. Furthermore, the incorporation of interactive media via the Ruff-Ruff, Tweet and Dave 2nd Screen App., allows viewers to become actively engaged with content and characters rather than simply observing what's on the TV.

    • Sarah & Duck

      The series focuses on the quieter but more lucid side of childhood, focusing on the day-to-day new experiences and learnings of growing up. No matter what they are doing, Sarah and Duck do it with an infectious wonder and curiosity that will have have everyone joining in on their simple but exciting adventures.

    • Stella and Sam

      The series learning goals are: social & emotional growth, positive citizenship, and divergent thinking. For Stella, the great outdoors is more than just a place–It's a playmate! Together, Stella leads her brother on explorations through the wild spaces beyond their back porch. As they explore the world each day, Stella and Sam share the joy of discovering life's biggest adventure of all…growing up!

    • Super Wings

      The series curriculum focuses on issues of creative problem solving and cultural diversity. As the Super Wings team zip around the globe helping those in need, the characters visit fifty-two cities in forty-five countries introducing young viewers to different food, language and culture. The Super Wings' travels offer young viewers a glimpse of the landscapes, languages, animals, food, clothing, games, music, and traditions that characterize different countries and cultures around the world.

    • Sydney Sailboat

      Sydney Sailboat features a loveable family of boats, trawlers and tugs that captures the wonder all young children feel. Sydney’s eager outlook and Zip’s determination embody that spirit of enthusiasm and curiosity as they set out to solve the unexpected challenges of their nautical world together. In every episode, the characters will approach a new challenge, problem or crisis, which will be resolved through adventure and play.

    • The Berenstain Bears

      The series incorporates three central learning areas: family and community, emotional development and choices & decision-making. Each episode focuses on an ordinary, everyday situation or “challenge” that young children may encounter in the home, with friends, in the community or at school (like jealousy, peer pressure, a new neighbor, a bad grade – or lack of “quality” family time). A path to resolving this common problem is provided as Brother and Sister (and viewers) see the consequences of the choices and decisions that they make. As each storyline progresses, family members interact to reveal solutions to conflicts and illustrate valuable life lessons.

    • The Chica Show

      The series aims to promote a creative approach towards typical preschool social-emotional challenges by reinforcing viewers interpersonal skills and coping strategies through lessons taught during each episode. The show also nurtures and reinforces creative and constructive play–an intrinsically motivating, hands-on way for viewers learn. Play is a guiding theme throughout the series given it is a vital component of early childhood–play is the way children practice skills and competencies, try new things and gain knowledge. The play modeled in The Chica Show features two aspects critical to creativity: the ability to transfer ways of solving problems between seemingly unrelated situations and the willingness to take risks and learn from failure.

    • The Good Night Show

      The series aims to wind your child down after a busy day with songs, stretches, spanish, sign language and storytelling. The series takes place in A organic, recycled, whimsical tree house, affectionately titled The You + Me Tree, overlooking the world of Sprout.

    • Sunny Side Up

      The series balances playful, spontaneous fun with interactive learning and creates a welcoming environment for preschoolers and their parents to spend some morning time together. In addition to introducing children to educational concepts through weekly themes, the concepts of calendars, days of the week, and weather are reinforced on a daily basis. Through birthday card submissions and other interactive activities, creativity and craft skills of real viewers are showcased on-air every day.

    • Tree Fu Tom

      The series is set in an enchanted world where movement creates magic. All of the Tree Fu "magic moves" that children are encouraged to copy are developed from therapeutic techniques that are used to help children with movement disorders like Dyspraxia, but are designed to assist and enhance the development of all participating children at a crucial time in their growth.

    • Zou

      The series aims to enable children to observe the processes of scientific inquiry and technological problem-solving in action, while also learning about physical science concepts and seeing positive attitudes towards learning. Each episode focuses on themes such as: family, growing up, relationships between family members and friends, learning how the world works and how to use one’s imagination. So while the emphasis of “Zou” the series is very much geared towards entertainment, there will, as a corollary, be a certain instructive content; the results of Zou’s explorations of the world and the boundaries which are set for him by his family, and the constricts of the extent of his knowledge will be there for the audience to experience and to investigate for themselves.

    • Zerby Derby

      The series educational philosophy aims to enable children to observe the processes of scientific inquiry and technological problem-solving, while also learning about physical science concepts and seeing positive attitudes towards learning, particularly curiosity and perseverance. Each episode makes valuable educational connections to science, technology, engineering and mathematics.

    Sprout, the 24-hour preschool TV channel, inspires families to take part in the possibilities that exist every single day by creating experiences and fun, meaningful content to help families learn, develop, be and grow together.
